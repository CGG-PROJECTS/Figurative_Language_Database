Picturebook Figurative Language Database

A database of picturebook and fiction examples of figurative language for teaching and curriculum

About This Resource

This database is part of the first large-scale study of figurative language in picturebooks. It contains ~68,617 figurative instances (metaphors, similes, personification, hyperbole) extracted from a massive corpus of over 5,700 picturebooks (~3.5 million words).

Picturebooks contain exceptionally high figurative language density — more than 5 times that of child-directed speech. This makes them a powerful (yet underexplored) resource for developing children’s metaphorical thinking, inference, and conceptual understanding.

How teachers can use it:

  • Find authentic examples for read-alouds and lessons
  • Filter by figurative type, source domain, or target domain (e.g., emotions, relationships)
  • Use AI explanations to help students understand meaning
  • Click 🔍 Google to quickly find read-aloud videos or the original book
  • Support social-emotional learning by exploring how emotions and relationships are described figuratively

This open educational resource bridges research and classroom practice. The full research paper is available upon request.
