About This Resource

Welcome to a research-based open resource for teachers. This database contains authentic examples of figurative language — metaphors, similes, personification, hyperbole, and more — from picturebooks and juvenile fiction.

Search, filter, and explore for classroom discussions, worksheets, and activities. Click the Google and AI-powered Explain buttons next to any entry explore further and/or source the text or a read-aloud.

You can also select examples and use the AI Teacher Tools tab to instantly generate classroom activities, explanations, related metaphors, and more.

Try searching for dream. You’ll see how one concept can be expressed in many rich and different ways — dreams as vast landscapes to explore, fragile objects to protect, destinations worth chasing, or things that can be built, shattered, or lost.

Use the dropdown filters to explore figurative language by domain: emotions, love, life and loss, health and illness, games and sport, human relationships, communication, and others.

This database was developed using AI (GPT-4.1) to extract and analyze a large collection of picturebooks that we developed, supplemented with 'juvenile fiction' from the Corpus of Contemporary American English (https://www.english-corpora.org/coca/).

Note: AI technology is not perfect! Each entry shows a confidence rating (5–7, where 7 is highest). Use the Google and Explain buttons to help verify and double-check borderline examples when needed.

The Domain labels (Source Domain/Target Domain) are designed to help find examples that share conceptual features. Figurative language often compares an abstract idea (the 'Target' or Topic) to something more concrete and familiar (the 'Source' or Vehicle). This helps writers express things like emotions, opinions, or complex ideas in a way readers can easily understand and everyday experiences. When I saw this database, I burst with joy. = Target (the abstract feeling): Communicating emotion of joy; Source (the concrete image): Physical movement/direction ("bursting").
